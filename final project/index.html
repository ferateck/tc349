THINGS NOT TO FLUSH

According to Wikipedia, the word "plumbing" comes from the Latin plumbum for lead, as pipes were once made from lead. Lead was the metal that was most commonly used for plumbing systems, until shortly after world war II when the switch to copper took place.

It's easy to toss stuff in the toilet, flush, and forget. But this not only negatively impacts the environment, but it's also painfully expensive to fix. Below is a list of a few things not to flush down the toilet. Sometimes things just don't go down there.

FEMININE PRODUCTS

Some experts claim that flushing tampons and sanitary napkins causes the majority of household clogs. The reason is because cotton snags easily, and if your home's plumbing pipes have any cracks or root infiltration, the cotton can quickly get caught in the line. After a few flushes, buildup can occur and you'll have a clog on your hands.

PETS

Yes, pets. Goldfish are commonly flushed, but small rodents (hamsters and gerbils) are also found in sewer systems. They're sturdy and they create clogs; consider a proper burial.

DIAPERS

Yes, people really do try to flush diapers down the toilet, and it's more common than you might think. Diapers will clog a toilet, or an outgoing line, in a heartbeat. They should always go in the trash

OTHER THINGS

Other things not to flush: chopsticks, articles of clothing such as underwear or shirts, paper towel (this can clump together and create big problems).
